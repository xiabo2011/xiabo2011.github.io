Zero Knowledge Proof Basic Concept

Zero Knowledge Proof

In cryptography, a zero-knowledge proof or zero-knowledge protocol is a method by which one party (the prover Peggy) can
prove to another party (the verifier Victor) that she knows a value x, without conveying any information apart from the
fact that she knows the value x.

Zero Knowledge Requirements

  • completeness
  • soundness
  • Zero-knowledge
